# Econometric Physics

*Intellectual explorations of an econometrician, trying to make sense of physics on a conceptual level.*

*By [Wayne Gao](https://wayneygao.com)*

I'm thankful to Gemini, ChatGPT, and Claude for discussing these ideas and composing these essays.*

---

## Overview

This project explores deep structural parallels between modern physics (quantum mechanics, general relativity) and econometrics/statistics. The central thesis is that the mathematical frameworks of identification and estimation — developed independently in econometrics — provide a precise and illuminating language for fundamental questions in physics, including the nature of the GR/QM conflict and what its resolution would require.

## Essays

### Essay 1: Quantum Mechanics and Semiparametric Estimation
Establishes a correspondence between QM and semiparametric statistics: Hilbert space geometry, Fourier uncertainty as Heisenberg's principle, sieve approximation, Riesz representers, and the distinction between regular and irregular functionals.

[[Full essay]](Essay_1.md) | [[Substack]](https://open.substack.com/pub/waynegao/p/quantum-physics-and-semiparametric?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web)

### Essay 2: General Relativity and Structural Identification
Reinterprets GR through the lens of structural econometrics: the Einstein field equations as a parametric structural model, the equivalence principle as local non-identification, gravitational lensing as partial identification, and the no-hair theorem as sharp identification. Argues that "fundamentally unidentifiable means nonexistent."

[[Full essay]](Essay_2.md) | [[Substack]](https://open.substack.com/pub/waynegao/p/general-relativity-and-econometric?utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web)

### Essay 3: An Econometric Reframing of the GR/QM Conflict
Synthesizes Essays 1 and 2 to reframe the GR/QM conflict as a DGP specification problem. Covers wave-particle duality as basis choice, entanglement as a global sieve property, measurement as estimation, the speed of light as a mixing condition, the information paradox, the arrow of time, and a six-point specification sheet for what reconciliation requires. Bridges toward a dependence-first axiomatization of spacetime geometry.
