# Econometric Physics — Series Summary

*Wayne Gao*

## Central Thesis

**Epistemology is ontology**: what exists is what can be identified (from the structural model) and estimated (from finite data). The mathematical frameworks of identification and estimation — developed independently in econometrics — provide a precise language for fundamental questions in physics. The GR/QM conflict is, at root, a DGP specification problem.

## Arc of the Series

The nine essays divide into four acts. Essays 1–2 establish the econometric vocabulary for physics. Essay 3 poses the central problem. Essays 4–7 build the solution — a layered architecture of structured information on a finite causal graph. Essay 8 audits the solution against the problem. Essay 9 closes the circle by returning to economics.

## Essay-by-Essay Outline

**Essay 1: Quantum Mechanics and Semiparametric Estimation.** Maps the mathematical structure of QM onto semiparametric statistics. Hilbert space is the function space; the wavefunction is a sieve expansion; Fourier uncertainty is Heisenberg's principle restated as a bias-variance tradeoff; the Riesz representer bridges linear functionals and inner products; regular vs. irregular functionals distinguish what can and cannot be estimated at root-n rate.

**Essay 2: General Relativity and Structural Identification.** Reads GR through structural econometrics. The Einstein field equations are a parametric structural model. The equivalence principle is local non-identification (Christoffel symbols are gauge-dependent; the Riemann tensor is identified). Gravitational lensing is partial identification with a mass-sheet degeneracy. The no-hair theorem is sharp identification from a minimal set of observables. The punchline: fundamentally unidentifiable means nonexistent.

**Essay 3: The GR/QM Conflict as a DGP Specification Problem.** Synthesizes Essays 1–2. Wave-particle duality is basis choice. Entanglement is a global sieve property. Measurement is estimation. The speed of light is a mixing condition. The information paradox and the arrow of time are reframed in econometric language. A six-point specification sheet defines what any reconciliation must deliver.

**Essay 4: The Finite Causal-Dependence Network (Spacetime Filtration I).** The constructive program begins. The primitive object is a finite causal DAG with local observables and overlapping σ-fields — the "spacetime filtration." Axiom L1 (conditional spacelike factorization) is the DAG global Markov property. Axiom L2 (cone-supported weak dependence) governs decay. Consequences: Markov equivalence as gauge invariance, faithfulness as the causal-set Hauptvermutung, the Markov blanket as causal horizon.

**Essay 5: From Finite Graphs to Geometry (Spacetime Filtration II).** Takes the finite DGP to its population limit via Benjamini–Schramm convergence, recovering a Haag–Kastler-type continuum net. The conformal class [g] emerges from the support of dependence (Malament 1977). The metric scale Ω²g emerges from bounded-Lipschitz decay rates. The Cauchy-temporal filtration gives the arrow of time. Complex conjugation is identified as the boundary of classical (real-valued) dependence theory.

**Essay 6: From Real Dependence to Complex Amplitude (Spacetime Filtration III).** Crosses that boundary. Argues for a clean separation of two layers: the global complex sieve specification (wavefunction) and the local real-valued information structure (filtrations, measurements). The algebraic lift from ℝ to ℂ is recast as equipping each node with an internal ℝ² and rotation operator J satisfying J² = −I — the "cumbersome honesty" of the 2×2 matrix. The characteristic function φ(t) / density f(x) duality from classical statistics guides the conceptual architecture: the specification layer is complex and global (like φ), the information layer is real and local (like f), and the Born map bridges them (like the inverse Fourier transform). Phase, interference, entanglement, and the Bell inequality are worked through in the rotation framework; J is the single structural ingredient behind all four.

**Essay 7: The Lifted Finite Framework (Spacetime Filtration IV).** Carries out the formal construction on the finite DAG. Each node's scalar observable is replaced by a 2-vector in ℝ² with rotation operator J. Two layers of σ-fields are defined: specification-layer (full amplitude) and information-layer (Born-projected). The lifted axiom system (L1′, L2′, Axiom J) is stated. Amplitude propagation via 2×2 propagator matrices gives interference as relative rotation between paths and entanglement as J-reflection pairing with antisymmetric covariance. The Bell–CHSH bound of 2 is the identification boundary of scalar models; the Tsirelson bound of 2√2 is what the rotational lifting achieves. The dependence framework of Essays 4–5 is recovered exactly as the Born projection.

**Essay 8: The Reconciliation — GR, QM, and the Arrow of Time.** Returns to Essay 3's specification sheet and audits each requirement. The three-way tension between GR, QM, and thermodynamics resolves as three views of the same layered structure: GR is the population structural model (reversible); QM specification is unitary amplitude propagation (reversible); the information layer is filtration growth plus Born projection (irreversible). The Markov blanket is developed as the event horizon. The information paradox is restated in filtration language: Hawking radiation as dependence leakage, the Page curve as mutual-information evolution. The arrow of time is local innovation realization. The "conflict" was a category error across layers. The Econometric Trinity names the resolution: the characteristic function φ(t) is QM (finite complex sieve, one term per quantum), the density f(x) is GR (smooth population limit), and the innovation process ε(x) is the second law (irreversible filtration growth). The sieve is finite because reality is quantized.

**Essay 9: Fundamental Axiom of Economics: Existence of an Economic Agent.** The capstone. Returns to economics with the question: why did an economist write about physics? Introduces Axiom E: the economic agent is a locally rooted node with accumulated filtration, internal objective architecture, and a decision rule. This axiom is irreducible — not derivable from physics. Consequences: individuality is ontological (not sentimental), bounded rationality is local rootedness, agency is structured stochastic self-determination. Markets are overlapping filtrations; prices are Riesz-representer-like summary statistics; equilibrium is a fixed-point compatibility condition. The specification/information gap reappears as asymmetric information; mechanism design is making the information layer informative about the specification layer. Physics and economics are two instantiations of structured information dynamics on a causal graph. The circle closes.

## Layered Architecture (Summary)

The series converges on a four-layer ontology:

1. **Structural grammar** — the causal DAG (finite) or manifold (continuum limit).
2. **Complex sieve specification** — the amplitude field {Y_v, J} with propagators; carries phase, interference, entanglement. Global in representation, not in information.
3. **Born-projected probabilities** — real-valued, locally estimable measurement outcomes.
4. **Local information and filtration** — the σ-fields that record realized observations; conditional independence, dependence decay, and geometric reconstruction live here.

The characteristic function φ(t) → density f(x) → data chain from classical statistics provides the guiding analogy: the specification layer is φ (complex, global), the information layer is f (real, local), and the realized filtration is the data.
